Blinking LEDs

Learn how to work with digital inputs and outputs with Facilino to make LEDs blink.

Required material.
  • Arduino Uno (and compatible) or Wemos D1R2/D1R32.
  • Arduino multisensor shield.

Exercises
  1. Blinking LEDs with a fixed time. Turn on and off red and blue LEDs of multisensor shield sequentially. They are connected to pins D12 (red) and 13 (blue). When one LED is on, the other LED must be off. The time delay between each sequence must be of 500ms. Hint: Repeat the sequence blue on and red off; wait 500ms; blue off and red on; wait 500ms.
  2. Blinking LEDs with varying frequency. Following the ideas of previous exercise make LEDs wait an amount of time between 100ms and 1000ms depending on the potentiometer position (connected to pin A0). Hint: Use the 'map' instruction to cast the raw value of the potentiometer (between 0 and 4095) to 100 and 1000.
  3. Blinking LEDs with increasing frequency. Following the ideas of previous exercise, now increase the blinking frequency (with a max. frequency of 10Hz) every time we push the push-button connected to pin D2. Hint: If the push-button D2 is LOW, then decrease the blinking time. Blinking time can be stored in a variable and when updated, we need to check that the minimum value is 50ms).
